Clinical Terminology Guide

ST Slope Explained

The ST segment of an ECG represents the period when the ventricles are depolarized. During stress testing:

  • Up (Upsloping): Typically normal, indicating healthy blood flow during exertion.
  • Flat (Flat): Often a sign of myocardial ischemia (restricted blood flow).
  • Down (Downsloping): Strong indicator of coronary artery disease.
Old Peak & ST Depression

ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest. Higher values (measured in mm) indicate higher degrees of cardiac strain and potential arterial blockages.

Resting ECG States
  • Normal: Normal resting electrical pattern.
  • ST: Having ST-T wave abnormality (T wave inversions and/or ST elevation/depression).
  • LVH: Showing probable or definite left ventricular hypertrophy.
Chest Pain Types
  • TA: Typical Angina (chest pain related to decreased blood flow).
  • ATA: Atypical Angina (chest discomfort without classic symptoms).
  • NAP: Non-Anginal Pain (sharp chest pain unrelated to the heart).
  • ASY: Asymptomatic (no chest pain symptoms, often critical to screen).
